FAQ
Frequently asked questions.
About the product
What is MinuteScribe?▼
MinuteScribe is a Windows application that turns a recording of your meeting into a complete, formatted set of minutes — saved as a Word document and ready for you to review and distribute.
What do the finished minutes look like?▼
MinuteScribe produces a professionally formatted Word document that follows the structure of formal meeting minutes — including the meeting date, attendees, agenda items, decisions made, and action items. You can edit the document in Word just like any other file.
Will MinuteScribe work for my type of organization?▼
MinuteScribe is designed for any organization that holds formal meetings and needs a written record — including churches, charities, nonprofit boards, HOAs, and small businesses. If your organization keeps meeting minutes, MinuteScribe can help.
Is my meeting content kept private?▼
Completely. MinuteScribe processes your recording directly on your own computer. No audio, no transcript, and no meeting content is ever sent to an outside server. Your meeting stays private.
About getting it running
What do I need to get started?▼
You need a Windows computer, a recording of your meeting, and a MinuteScribe licence code. That is all. No additional software subscriptions are required.
Does MinuteScribe work on my computer?▼
MinuteScribe runs on Windows 10 and Windows 11. If your computer runs either of these, you are good to go.
How do I get my recording onto my computer?▼
It depends on how you made your recording. Our Getting Started guide walks you through the most common scenarios — including transferring a recording from your phone and saving a recording from Zoom or Teams.
I use the free version of Google Meet. Can I still use MinuteScribe?▼
Yes. The free version of Google Meet does not include a built-in recording feature, but free Chrome extensions are available that can record your meeting audio. MinuteScribe works with the audio formats these extensions produce. See the User Questions section for guidance on setting up a Chrome extension for Google Meet recording.
About using it
How long does it take to produce the finished minutes?▼
It depends on the length of your recording. A two hour meeting typically takes around ten minutes to process. MinuteScribe will show you its progress while it works — a good time to step away and get a coffee.
Where will the finished document be saved on my computer?▼
MinuteScribe saves your finished minutes in the Minutes folder, which is accessible directly from the MinuteScribe application. Just click the Open Minutes Folder button.
What if the minutes do not look quite right?▼
MinuteScribe does its best to produce accurate, well structured minutes — but no automated tool is perfect. Speaker names in particular may occasionally need correction. The finished minutes are saved as a standard Word document. You can edit, format, and save them exactly as you would any other Word file.
About buying it
How does purchasing work?▼
MinuteScribe is purchased as a bundle of credits rather than a monthly subscription. You buy once, use your credits as you need them, and top up when your balance runs low. There are no recurring charges.
What are credits and how do I get more?▼
Each time MinuteScribe generates a set of minutes it uses one credit. Your remaining credit balance is always shown in the application. When your balance runs low MinuteScribe will let you know. You can top up at any time by visiting www.minutescribe.ca and clicking Purchase Credits.
What if I need help?▼
If you cannot find the answer you need in our Getting Started guide or User Questions section, you are welcome to contact us directly at support@minutescribe.ca. We respond within two business days.

Privacy Policy▼
Effective date: 31 May 2026

THE SHORT VERSION
MinuteScribe is built around a simple privacy principle: your audio recordings never leave your computer. This policy explains exactly what happens to your data when you use MinuteScribe.

1. WHO WE ARE
MinuteScribe is a Windows desktop application developed and operated by Franklin Wong, carrying on business as MinuteScribe (“MinuteScribe,” “we,” “us”). You can reach us at support@minutescribe.ca.

2. WHAT MINUTESCRIBE DOES — AND DOES NOT — COLLECT
MinuteScribe does not collect, store, or transmit: your audio recordings; your name, email address, or any account information; usage statistics or analytics; or any data in the background.

The only data that leaves your computer is the text transcript of your meeting, and only when you choose to generate minutes. See Section 3 for details.

3. WHAT HAPPENS WHEN YOU GENERATE MINUTES
When you click “Generate Minutes,” MinuteScribe does the following: (1) Your audio file is transcribed into text on your own computer using a local speech-recognition model (Whisper). The audio file never leaves your machine. (2) The text transcript is sent to the Anthropic Claude API, operated by Anthropic, PBC, for the sole purpose of producing your formatted meeting minutes. Anthropic does not use API inputs to train its models. The transcript is processed and the result is returned to your computer — it is not stored or retained by MinuteScribe. (3) The finished minutes are returned to your computer and displayed in the application.

No other network communication takes place. MinuteScribe makes no background connections of any kind.

4. ANTHROPIC’S HANDLING OF TRANSCRIPT DATA
For completeness, Anthropic’s data practices are governed by their Privacy Policy, available at https://www.anthropic.com/legal/privacy. We have no control over Anthropic’s data practices and are not responsible for them.

5. YOUR RESPONSIBILITY FOR MEETING PARTICIPANTS
Meeting transcripts may contain personal information about people who attended your meeting. You are responsible for ensuring that your use of MinuteScribe complies with any applicable privacy laws and that meeting participants have been made aware that their words may be transcribed and processed. If you are unsure whether your use of MinuteScribe complies with your organisation’s privacy obligations, please seek appropriate advice.

6. CANADIAN PRIVACY LAW (PIPEDA)
MinuteScribe is operated from Canada and complies with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA). Because MinuteScribe collects no personal information itself — and because the only data transmitted is the meeting transcript sent to Anthropic at your explicit direction — your PIPEDA rights regarding MinuteScribe are limited in practical scope. Getting Started
From installation to your first set of finished minutes.
MinuteScribe is designed to be straightforward to install and use. This guide walks you through everything in five parts. If you get stuck at any point, help is available at support@minutescribe.ca.
Part 1 — Installing▼
Step 1
Download the installer
After your purchase, you will receive an email containing your download link. Click the link to download the MinuteScribe installer file — called MinuteScribe_Setup.exe — to your computer. Your browser will save it to your Downloads folder.
Step 2
Windows will show a security warning — here is what to do
When you double-click the installer, Windows will display a security warning that says “Windows protected your PC.” This is normal and expected for newly released software. It does not mean MinuteScribe is harmful.
  1. Click More info on the warning screen
  2. Click Run anyway
  3. The installer will open normally
Step 3
Run the installer
The MinuteScribe installer will open as a standard Windows wizard. Click Next through the steps and then click Install. When installation is complete click Finish. A MinuteScribe shortcut will appear on your desktop automatically.
Part 2 — First-time Setup▼
Step 4
Enter your organisation name
The first time MinuteScribe is run, a text file will open in Notepad. You are asked to type your organisation’s name. Click Save in Notepad to save the text file. MinuteScribe will use this name in every set of minutes it produces.
Step 5
Enter your personnel list
The first time MinuteScribe is run, a text file will open in Notepad. You are asked type the names of your board members or regular attendees, one per line. Click Save in Notepad to save the text file. Close Notepad. MinuteScribe includes this list in the minutes.
Step 6
Enter your licence code
Double-click the MinuteScribe shortcut on your desktop. The licence code is included in your purchase confirmation email. Type or paste the code and click Redeem Credit Code.
Part 3 — Getting Your Recording Ready▼
MinuteScribe needs your meeting recording. Choose your recording scenario below and follow those steps. If you recorded on your phone, also follow the LocalSend transfer steps at the end.
Getting the audio recording onto your computer is the one step where you may need help from someone. The process becomes familiar quickly. Ask a tech-savvy colleague to help install LocalSend if needed — instructions are in the User Questions section.
Step 1 — iPhone
Record your meeting
Open the Voice Memos app before the meeting begins. Tap the red record button to start. Tap Stop when the meeting ends.
Step 2 — iPhone
Save the recording to your phone’s Files folder
  1. Open Voice Memos and tap your recording to select it
  2. Tap the three dot menu (•••)
  3. Tap Share, then Save to Files
  4. Navigate to Downloads, supply a filename, tap Save
Then continue to the LocalSend steps below.
Step 1 — Android
Record your meeting
Open the Recorder app before the meeting begins. Tap the record button to start. Tap Stop when the meeting ends.
Step 2 — Android
Save the recording to Downloads
  1. Open the Recorder app and tap your recording to select it
  2. Tap the three dot menu, then the Share icon
  3. Tap Next to select the Audio (.m4a) file, then tap Files
  4. Supply a filename and save to Downloads
Then continue to the LocalSend steps below.
Step 1 — Zoom
Save your Zoom recording
Have the host turn ON recording at the start. Stop when the meeting ends. The recording is saved automatically to the Zoom folder on your computer. Rename it, then go to Part 4.
Step 2 — Teams
Save your Teams recording
Have the host turn ON recording at the start. Stop when the meeting ends. The recording is saved to OneDrive. Download it to your Downloads folder, rename it, then go to Part 4.
iPhone and Android users — Transfer using LocalSend
Step 3
Open LocalSend on both devices
Open LocalSend on your computer first, then on your phone. Both must be on the same WiFi network.
Step 4
Send the recording from your phone
  1. Tap Send, then File, then Audio
  2. Select your recording and tap your computer name
Step 5
Accept on your computer
Click Accept when the request appears, then Done when complete.
▶ Watch: LocalSend file transfer demonstration
Step 6
Rename the recording if needed
Open MinuteScribe, click Select Audio File, find the file in Downloads, right-click and Rename it to something like 2026-05-14-Meeting.
Part 4 — Running MinuteScribe▼
Step 7
Select your recording file
Double-click the MinuteScribe shortcut. Click Select Audio File, navigate to Downloads, click your recording, and click Open.
Step 8
Wait for processing
MinuteScribe begins processing immediately. A two hour meeting takes approximately ten minutes. Do not close the application while it is working.
Step 9
Review your finished document
Your completed minutes open automatically in Word. Read through carefully before using as an official record. Speaker names may need correction. To find your minutes later, click Open Minutes Folder.
Part 5 — Managing Credits▼
Step 10
Checking your balance
Your remaining credit balance is always shown in the MinuteScribe application. Each time MinuteScribe produces a set of minutes, one credit is used. When your balance reaches three credits or fewer, MinuteScribe will remind you to top up.
Step 11
Topping up when your balance is low
Open the Credits menu and select Buy Credits. Complete your purchase and enter the new licence code by selecting Redeem Credit Code. Your new credits are added immediately.

Installing and setting up LocalSend
How do I install LocalSend on my computer and phone?▼
LocalSend transfers your meeting recording over your WiFi network. The file never leaves your local network. Choose your device below.
  1. Go to localsend.org and click Download
  2. Click EXE (Installer) to download
  3. Double-click the installer, select Install for all users, click Yes
  4. Accept defaults, add desktop shortcut, click Next → Install → Finish
  5. Open LocalSend from your desktop
  6. Click Settings → Receive → Save to folder → Downloads
  7. Click Select Folder, then Receive — LocalSend is ready
  1. Go to the Google Play Store, search localsend
  2. Install the app by Tien Do Nam
  3. Open LocalSend on your computer first, then on your phone
  4. Grant permission to access Music and audio files — tap Allow
  5. LocalSend on Android is ready to use
  1. Go to the Apple App Store, search localsend
  2. Install the app by Tien Do Nam
  3. Open LocalSend on your computer first, then on your iPhone
  4. Tap Allow when asked to connect to devices on your network
  5. LocalSend on iPhone is ready to use

Computer requirements and Google Meet
What are the minimum computer requirements to run MinuteScribe?▼
MinuteScribe requires Windows 10 or Windows 11. The computer should have at least 8GB of RAM. A computer with only 4GB of RAM may struggle with the audio transcription step. A mid-range computer purchased in the last five years should be sufficient.
How can I record a meeting held on the free version of Google Meet?▼
The free version of Google Meet does not include a built-in recording feature. Free Chrome extensions are available that can record your meeting audio. MinuteScribe works with the audio formats these extensions produce.

Cloud storage for large audio files
How do I set up cloud storage to transfer a recording that is too large to send by email?▼
If a recording exceeds 200MB it cannot be transferred by email. Google Drive is a free cloud storage service that can be used to transfer large files between your phone and computer.
